Golf Swing Reference Guide

Use these checkpoints when recording or reviewing your swing.

Ten-Step Swing Breakdown
1. Setup
  • Stance: Feet shoulder-width apart for irons and slightly wider for woods. Toes flare 10°–15°, knees softly flexed, hips hinged, and spine neutral.
  • Grip: Overlap, interlock, or ten-finger grip with medium pressure. Left hand faces the target, right hand covers the left thumb, and both “V” shapes aim between the chin and right shoulder.
  • Ball position: Short irons in the middle, long irons and fairway woods inside the left heel, driver off the lead heel.
  • Alignment: Clubface square to the target, body lines parallel, head stays behind the ball, eyes locked on the ball.
Setup
2. Takeaway
  • Shoulders, arms, hands, and club move away together. Keep the clubhead low to the ground and allow the wrists to hinge naturally.
Takeaway
3. Backswing
  • Turn the shoulders around the spine, lift the arms, shift pressure to the inside of the trail foot, and keep the trail knee stable while creating wrist lag.
Backswing
4. Top of Backswing
  • Left shoulder touches or passes under the chin, club points toward the target line, weight is stacked over the trail leg, right elbow stays bent, left arm long, spine angle preserved, and head steady.
Top of Backswing
5. Downswing
  • Lower body initiates: plant the lead heel, shift pressure to the lead side, and rotate the hips aggressively while maintaining wrist lag so energy sequences from the ground up.
Downswing
6. Pre-Impact
  • Hips open significantly, shoulders square up, head remains behind the ball, pressure builds into the lead leg, the lead leg extends, and wrists begin to unhinge.
Pre-Impact
7. Impact
  • Hands are ahead of the ball, lead wrist flat, clubface square to the target, and contact is made with the sweet spot while compressing the ball.
Impact
8. Release
  • Wrists release stored energy, clubhead accelerates through the strike zone, torso keeps rotating, arms extend fully, trail shoulder points at the target, and pressure remains on the lead side.
Release
9. Follow-Through
  • Body keeps rotating, weight transfers fully to the lead foot, and the club stays on plane for a smooth, balanced finish.
Follow-Through
10. Finish
  • Chest faces the target, trail shoulder sits lower than the lead shoulder, trail foot is on its toe, the club wraps behind the body, and the swing ends in a tall, relaxed balance pose.
Finish
Setup Reference Lines
Why use reference lines?
  • Reference lines make it easier to inspect weight distribution, hip sway, and head movement over time.
  • They give every recording the same visual baseline from the “1 o’clock” camera angle used in the demo footage.
1. Heel Line
  • Runs through both heels and highlights lateral sway or inconsistent foot pressure.
Heel Line
2. Knee & Ankle Lines
  • Two stacked lines that show whether the lower body remains stable while loading and unloading.
Knee & Ankle Lines
3. Armpit–Knee–Ankle Line
  • A vertical line that confirms the arms hang naturally so the armpit, knee, and ankle align in a straight column.
Armpit–Knee–Ankle
4. Head Position Line
  • Marks where the head should remain throughout the swing so you can detect swaying or dipping.
Head Position
5. Hands Reference Line
  • Aligns with the hands at setup and highlights whether they maintain height while the shoulders turn.
Hands Line
6. Ankle Reference
  • Tracks ankle flex and ensures the lower body remains grounded before pushing off.
Ankle Line
7. Hip Vertical Reference
  • Helps spot excessive hip sway or loss of posture by highlighting the hip pocket at setup.
Hip Vertical
8. Back-Swing Direction Line
  • Shows the recommended path for the club and shoulders during the backswing to avoid coming too far inside or outside.
Back Direction
9. Front-Swing Direction Line
  • Extends toward the target to evaluate whether the club exits on plane during the follow-through.
Front Direction
